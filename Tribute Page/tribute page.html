Albert Einstein

A great Scientist and Physicist


Here is the timeline of the Albert Einstein

  • 1879 - Born in Ulm, Kingdom of Wurttemberg, German Empire
  • 1887 - received advanced primary and secondary school education
  • 1895 - He sat the entrance examinations for the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zürich.
  • 1900 - Einstein's paper "Folgerungen aus den Capillaritätserscheinungen" ("Conclusions from the Capillarity Phenomena") was published in the journal Annalen der Physik.
  • 1903 - Married with Maric.
  • 1904 - First son, Hans Albert Einstein, was born in Bern, Switzerland.
  • 1905 - Annus Mirabilis papers
  • 1908 - He was recognized as a leading scientist and was appointed lecturer at the University of Bern.
  • 1910 - Their second son, Eduard, was born in Zürich.
  • 1911 - He made his new theory of general relativity.
  • 1912 - He was professor of theoretical physics at the ETH Zurich, where he taught analytical mechanics and thermodynamics.
  • 1914 - He returned to the German Empire after being appointed director of the Kaiser Wilhelm Institute for Physics (1914–1932) and a professor at the Humboldt University of Berlin.
  • 1918 - He was appointed president of the German Physical Society.
  • 1919 - Divorced on 14 February 1919, having lived apart for five years
  • 1919 - He married Elsa Löwenthal.
  • 1920 - He became a Foreign Member of the Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences.
  • 1922 - He was awarded the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics "for his services to Theoretical Physics, and especially for his discovery of the law of the photoelectric effect
  • 1925 - He received the Copley Medal from the Royal Society.
  • 1929 - Receive Mac Plank Medal
  • 1946 - He visited Lincoln University in Pennsylvania, a historically black college, where he was awarded an honorary degree
  • 1955 - Died at the age of 76
